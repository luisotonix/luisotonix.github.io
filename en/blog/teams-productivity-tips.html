Optimizing Microsoft Teams for Productivity

üë§ Lu√≠s Otoni üìÖ November 20, 2025 ‚è±Ô∏è 6 min read üìÅ Microsoft 365

Microsoft Teams has become the most used collaboration platform in corporate environments. However, many users utilize only a fraction of its capabilities. In this article, I share practical tips to boost your productivity with Teams.

1. Essential Keyboard Shortcuts

Mastering keyboard shortcuts can save hours over the month. Here are the most useful ones:

  • Ctrl + E: Global search - find messages, files, and people quickly
  • Ctrl + Shift + M: Mute/unmute during calls
  • Ctrl + Shift + O: Toggle camera on/off
  • Ctrl + Shift + A: Reply to last message
  • Ctrl + N: Start new chat
  • Ctrl + / : Complete list of shortcuts

2. Organize Your Teams and Channels

Use Private Channels

For sensitive discussions or specific projects, use private channels. They allow granular control over who has access to the content.

Favorite Important Channels

Click the three dots next to the channel and select "Favorite". Favorited channels appear at the top of the list and you receive notifications for all messages.

Hide Inactive Teams

Keep your sidebar clean by hiding teams you don't use frequently. Click the three dots on the team ‚Üí Hide.

3. Master Mentions

Use mentions strategically to get attention when needed:

  • @Name: Mentions specific person
  • @team: Notifies all team members
  • @channel: Notifies everyone who favorited the channel

Tip: Use sparingly! Excessive mentions cause notification fatigue.

4. Smart Notification Settings

Configure notifications to avoid unnecessary distractions:

Custom Notifications per Channel

Click the three dots on the channel ‚Üí Channel notifications ‚Üí Choose:

  • All activity: For critical channels
  • Custom: Only mentions and replies
  • Off: For informational channels

Status and Focus Time

  • Set "Quiet hours" to not be disturbed outside work hours
  • Use "Do not disturb" status during focused work
  • Schedule automatic statuses (Available during day, Away at night)

5. Chat Power Users

Format Messages

Use markdown for clearer messages:

  • **bold** for emphasis
  • *italic* for light highlighting
  • ``` for code
  • Numbered and bullet point lists

Saved Messages

Save important messages for future reference. Click the three dots ‚Üí Save this message. Access your saved messages from your profile icon.

Quick Reactions

Use emoji reactions instead of replying "OK" or "Thanks". Saves time and reduces message clutter.

6. Advanced Meeting Features

Backgrounds and Effects

  • Use background blur to keep focus on you
  • Choose professional backgrounds for important meetings
  • Set default background in settings

Together Mode

For team meetings, Together Mode creates the feeling of being in the same room, increasing engagement.

Breakout Rooms

In training or workshops, use breakout rooms to divide participants into smaller groups for discussions.

Recording and Transcription

  • Record important meetings (notify participants!)
  • Enable automatic transcription to generate meeting minutes
  • Recordings are available in SharePoint/OneDrive

7. App Integration

Planner and To Do

Add Planner tab in channels to manage team tasks. Your personal tasks sync with Microsoft To Do.

OneNote

Create OneNote tab in channels for collaborative documentation and meeting notes.

Power Automate

Create flows to automate workflows:

  • Post message when new item is created in SharePoint
  • Notify team about new emails in shared mailbox
  • Create approvals directly in Teams

Third-party Apps

Integrate tools your team already uses: Trello, Jira, GitHub, Salesforce, etc.

8. Advanced Search

Teams has powerful search that few explore:

  • from:person - Messages from someone specific
  • in:channel - Search in specific channel
  • hasfile - Only messages with attachments
  • subject:text - Email subject (also searches Outlook)

9. Efficient Mobile Mode

The Teams mobile app is powerful:

  • Use push notifications to not miss urgent messages
  • Reply to messages with audio when on the move
  • Join meetings with audio only when necessary
  • Use location sharing to inform arrival

10. Etiquette Tips

Respect Others' Time

  • Don't send messages outside business hours (use schedule send)
  • Respect "Do not disturb" status
  • Avoid unnecessary meetings - chat may be sufficient

Clear Communication

  • Use subject lines in long messages
  • Be concise and objective
  • Use threads to keep conversations organized

Conclusion

Microsoft Teams is a powerful tool that, when used well, can transform your team's collaboration. The tips in this article are just the beginning - explore, experiment, and find the workflow that works best for you and your team.

Remember: productivity isn't about using all the features, but using the right features in the right way for your specific context.
